BOOK REVIEW


     The Personal MBA

  • Author Name:Josh Kaufman

  • Publisher: PENGUIN PORTFOLIO

  • Publication date: 2010

     Summary: I personally would say this is like one of those self-help books with styles borrowed from Dale Carnegie. The books starts with the author's claim      that he is not an MBA grad and that you do not need to have an MBA to be able to leverage your skill. The topics are the most obvious ones. I was totally stuck      with 76 pages devoted just to prove his point that MBA degree is overvalued. If you are into self-help, you may find this helpful. But, I could not find any   valuable point on what is totally an obvious subject that the author seemed to be highlighting.

Rating: 4.3/5.

     21 lessons for 21st Century

  • Author Name:Yuval Noah Harari

  • Publisher: Spiegel & Grau, Jonathan Cape

  • Publication date: 2018

     Summary:The review aggregator website Book Marks reported that 44% of critics gave the book a "rave" review, whilst 11% of the critics "panned" the      book. The rest of the critics expressed either "positive" (22%) or "mixed" (22%) impressions, based on a sample of nine reviews.[4] The book had articles and       reviews published by The New York Times, The Economist,[5] Financial Times, The Guardian, New Statesman, and The Times. The book has also received      negative reviews. Gavin Jacobson in the New Statesman sees it as "a study thick with promise and thin in import" with advice "either too vague or too hollow to      provide any meaningful guidance."[8] In The Times, Gerard DeGroot writes: "The author of Sapiens is good at identifying the crises to come but his                    syrupy platitudes are no answer."

Rating: 4.2/5.
